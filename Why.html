Diverse Abilities

People may be born with disabilities or acquire them sometime in their life. There's a broad spectrum of disabilites and it's important to try to accommodate as many of them as possible. These are just a few of the numerous diverse abilities out there.

Health Conditions:

Stamina, dexterity, or concentration are some health conditions that affect a person's physical ability to operate their device. This could seriously limit their extent of their use of digital technology

Multiple Disabilites:

A person could have a collective amount of disabilities that could damper their functions. With this in mind, their approach to the use of technology narrows.

Age-Related Impairments

The attitude towards treating other disabilites' accommodations is typically the same for people with age-related impairments. A difference though is the median of assistive technology and the use of digital technology.

Explaination

Universal design should be a core principle to everything created, especially websites.

The idea of why it should be a core principle is that when anybody can use it everybody will use it. So, being well aware of what kind of diverse abilities are out there is important. It's important to understand what kind of struggles people go through to use websites. For example, a person with a health condition that is unable to operate a desktop so trying a mobile setup could be extremely helpful.

Expanding accessibility to a broader audience inherently increases the likelihood that the project will be widely utilized. When looking at the United States, there's about 100 million people who have a disability. Creating accessible things, like websites, let's those people have the same opportunity as everyone else.
