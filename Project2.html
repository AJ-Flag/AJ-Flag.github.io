About this page

Why?

Universal design should be a core principle to everything created, especially websites. The idea of why it should

be a core principle is that when anybody can use it everybody will use it. Reaching to all audiences of people will make almost

get attention. For example, closed captions began with the intention to assist people who have hearing impairments but now

people use it when they're in a noisy environment, trying to learn a new language, people that simply want to read along, etc.

In general, it's the right thing to do.

What?

What does it mean to design universally? It means to design with diversity in mind to begin with. Diversity for those

who're able and disabled. A design that can be accused to anyone who wishes to use it and that doesn't disclude a certain type

or group of people, it only includes everybody. It's something that is considered morally good and is a practice of good design.

How?

How to design a website technically? To design a good website for everybody it means to make sure there's nothing that makes

it difficult for people to view and technically use the site. For the visablly impaired, there should be nothing that causes any strain

when trying to physically view a website. Next is designing any page so a screen reader can work effectively on it.

Finally, mapping out all pages to a site is crucial so nothing looks confusing and can be easily interpreted.
